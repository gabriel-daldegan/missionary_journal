## Story

**As a** user with a completed profile,
**I want** to land on a memory-first timeline,
**so that** I can immediately see and revisit memories by when they happened.

## Acceptance Criteria

1. The timeline home is the first meaningful memory product screen after profile setup.
2. Records are ordered by experience date or period start date, not creation date.
3. Records are grouped under month separators.
4. Timeline cards show date or period, optional location, meaningful excerpt, simple tags, photo count when available, and a clear action to open the full record.
5. The empty state encourages diary record creation without using album-generation language.
6. The timeline is not rendered as a table, admin list, Filament resource, marketing hero, blog-card feed, masonry layout, map, or complex animation surface.

## Integration Verification

1. **IV1:** Timeline queries are scoped by tenant membership.
2. **IV2:** Timeline cards do not expose records from other tenants.
3. **IV3:** Tests verify ordering, month grouping, empty state, and cross-tenant exclusion.

## Implementation Interpretation

Story 1.6 turns the existing Story 1.5 timeline baseline into the first real
memory home after profile setup. Story 1.5 already provides tenant-scoped
timeline routing, diary record detail links, and date-based ordering. This
story should refine that surface into a vertical month-grouped timeline home
with useful cards, empty-state copy, and focused query/service boundaries.

The story must remain a timeline-home slice. It must not implement trip/period
record creation, private media upload, media delivery, timeline filters, public
sharing, visibility selectors, Filament resources, admin tables, or a marketing
hero surface.

Ordering must use the record's timeline date: `experience_date` for diary
records and `period_start_date` for future period records. Period creation
remains Story 1.7, so this story may prepare the query/service boundary for
period records without exposing period creation UI.

## Scope Boundaries

- Use Story 1.5's merged detail/edit/timeline baseline.
- Keep the timeline route as `memories.timeline` under `/memories/{tenant:uuid}`.
- Keep profile setup, account, billing, dashboard, invitation, and workspace-management flows unchanged except for regression fixes.
- Do not add media upload, media storage, media routes, or private media delivery.
- Do not rely on public media URLs or public blog-media assumptions.
- Show photo count only when record media/count data is already available; otherwise keep the card stable without photo UI claims.
- Do not implement filters by date, tag, or location. That scope belongs to Story 1.9.
- Do not implement trip/period record creation. That scope belongs to Story 1.7.
- Do not add masonry, map browsing, complex animation, public sharing, visibility toggles, or album-generation language.

### Code Quality Assessment

PASS for Story 1.6 after architect review. The implementation now keeps the timeline read boundary in `MemoryTimelineService`, scopes records by tenant, groups records by localized month labels, renders a vertical customer-facing Livewire timeline, and preserves the route/profile middleware boundary from prior stories.

Architect review found two issues before issuing the gate. First, PHPStan reported that the timeline service PHPDoc promised an `EloquentCollection` payload while `groupBy()->values()` returned the broader Laravel collection shape. Second, the timeline card used CSS line clamping on the full body, which visually shortened the card but still sent the full record body in the timeline HTML instead of a real excerpt. Both issues were fixed and covered before the final PASS.

### Refactoring Performed

- **File**: `app/Services/MemoryTimelineService.php`
  - **Change**: Adjusted the grouped payload PHPDoc to `Collection<int, MemoryRecord>` and made timeline date resolution type-aware.
  - **Why**: PHPStan required the real collection contract, and the story requires diary records to use `experience_date` while period records use `period_start_date`.
  - **How**: Keeps the service contract honest and prevents future period metadata from changing diary timeline ordering.

- **File**: `app/Models/MemoryRecord.php`
  - **Change**: Added `timelineDate()`, `timelineExcerpt()`, and `timelinePhotoCount()` helpers.
  - **Why**: Keeps timeline card derivation out of Blade and centralizes record-level display decisions.
  - **How**: Gives the service and view a small, typed API for timeline date, excerpt, and photo count.

- **File**: `resources/views/livewire/memory/memory-timeline.blade.php`
  - **Change**: Rendered a real `Str::limit()` excerpt instead of outputting the full body with visual-only line clamping.
  - **Why**: AC4 asks for a meaningful excerpt, and the card should not include the full record body in timeline HTML.
  - **How**: Keeps the card concise while preserving the detail route as the full-record surface.

- **File**: `tests/Feature/Services/Memory/MemoryTimelineServiceTest.php`
  - **Change**: Added coverage for type-aware timeline date resolution.
  - **Why**: Prevents diary records with reserved period fields from being grouped or ordered by the wrong date.
  - **How**: Verifies period records group by `period_start_date` and diary records group by `experience_date`.

- **File**: `tests/Feature/Livewire/Memory/MemoryTimelineTest.php`
  - **Change**: Added coverage that long timeline cards render an excerpt without the full body tail.
  - **Why**: Locks AC4 to server-rendered output, not only CSS appearance.
  - **How**: Proves the opening text appears and the hidden full-body tail is absent from the timeline response.
